Foundation: The Cruzeiro Esporte Clube was founded in 1921 under the name Società Sportiva Palestra Itália by an Italian community living in Belo Horizonte. In 1942, due to the conflict of World War II, the club was forced to change its name because Italy was one of the countries that formed the Axis Powers. At that time, the government decreed that football teams were prohibited from using names that referred to any of the Axis nations (Germany, Italy, and Japan). For this reason, on October 7, 1942, the club adopted the name it is known by today, Cruzeiro Esporte Clube, thus beginning a new phase in its history. The name "Cruzeiro" is a tribute to the constellation Crux (Southern Cross), which holds symbolic importance in Brazil, appearing on the national flag and in the country's coat of arms.

This nickname is also because foxes eat roosters and chickens.

Stadium: The Mineirão Stadium, located in Belo Horizonte, is one of the main stadiums in Brazil. Cruzeiro Esporte Clube plays its home matches there and has a strong connection to the stadium, where it has won many important titles.

Titles: Among its main achievements, we can list four Campeonato Brasileiro Série A titles (1966, 2003, 2013, 2014), six Copa do Brasil titles (1993, 1996, 2000, 2003, 2017, 2018)—making it the club with the most Copa do Brasil titles and two Copa Libertadores titles (1976, 1997).)
